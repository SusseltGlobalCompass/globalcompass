Community Guidelines

The standards every post on GlobalCompass is held to, before the Forum opens.

Personal experiences, not verified ratings
Posts in School Experiences reflect one family's personal experience at a point in time. They are not independently verified by GlobalCompass and are not an official rating or ranking of any school. We ask every contributor to describe specific, factual experiences rather than general claims about a school's overall quality.
What we expect from every post
  • No obscene, vulgar, or sexually explicit language, in any language.
  • No personal attacks, harassment, or insults directed at any individual, including staff or other families.
  • No defamatory claims — statements presented as fact about a school, individual, or organisation that could harm their reputation and cannot be substantiated.
  • No sharing of another person's private or identifying information without consent.
  • No discriminatory language relating to nationality, religion, race, or any other protected characteristic.
  • No spam, advertising, or commercial solicitation outside of approved channels.
How moderation works

Every post passes through an automated language filter before publishing. Posts containing flagged language are automatically hidden and queued for human review before they can appear publicly.

GlobalCompass reserves the right to remove any post, at any time, that does not meet these guidelines, without prior notice. Repeated violations may result in a contributor losing posting access entirely.

Posting requires a verified GlobalCompass account. Anonymous posting is not permitted, so that standards can be properly maintained across the community.

A note on local law

GlobalCompass operates from the United Arab Emirates, where publishing defamatory or false statements about a person or organisation can carry legal consequences for the author, regardless of intent. These guidelines exist to protect contributors as much as the community itself — when in doubt, describe your specific experience rather than make a general claim.
